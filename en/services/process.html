Your Track.

Pick the track closest to your situation, follow the timeline. If you can clearly say "I'll get stuck at month X doing Y" — you can probably run it yourself. If you can only say "this looks complicated" — that's why OPC exists.

Track 1: Renovation Foreman — Independent Contracting

Manual labor / low formal education · Shenzhen · Eligible subsidies: social insurance ¥890/mo + rental ¥1,560/mo

Month 1 — Survival

{Quit job / leave umbrella company → lose stable income, anxiety kicks in. Register sole proprietorship (visit business bureau / get seal / open bank account) → 7-14 days, don't know where to start, materials rejected. Find work (WeChat Moments / property offices) → no response, getting rejected. Income: ¥0}

Month 2 — Grinding

{Visit 10 property offices for partnerships → 9 rejections. First inquiry → no idea how to price. First deal signed ¥30K → don't know how to write a contract. Work starts → supplier cheats on materials, workers demand more pay. Final payment → client nitpicks and delays. Income: ¥30K (gross profit ¥9,000)}

Month 3 — Stabilizing

{First deal final payment delayed 10 days. Discover startup subsidies → Shenzhen conditions complex, can't understand. First subsidy submission → rejected. Fix and resubmit → waiting. Income: ¥30K (gross profit ¥7,000)}

Month 4 — Bottleneck

{Averaging 1-2 deals/month, ¥30-50K revenue. Can't figure out the accounting. Subsidy finally arrives ¥2,450. Afraid to quit, not sure if next month will have deals. 4-month net income: ~¥26K}

Track 3 (Failed): Solopreneur Coach

Chengdu · Knowledge-pay track · ¥30K savings, gave up after 5 months

Month 0 — Decision

{6 months as community ops at a paid knowledge group. Thinks they've figured it out. Quits to become "solopreneur coach." Posts 3 WeChat Moments: "Coaching ¥9,999/person." Responses: 0}

Month 1 — Self-Doubt

Posts on Zhihu "Top 10 Solopreneur Mistakes." 37 views. 3 posts, stops posting. Considers dropping price to ¥999 to get first client. OPC advice: "Run a real business first, then teach." — They don't buy it.

Month 2 — Breaking Point

Savings ¥30K→¥15K. Starts selling ¥9.9 "startup resource pack." Sells 17 copies, ¥168 revenue. 2 people publicly call them a scammer. Starts losing sleep.

Month 3-5 — Loans→Quit→Back to Square One

{Borrows ¥12,000 online loans. Cancels business license. Sends resumes. Back to old career doing community ops ¥8,000/mo. Never mentions the word "startup" again.}

The lesson of this case: don't teach what you haven't done. The gap between knowing and doing is an ocean.

How Should Your Track Go?

Picking the right track beats pure effort. Pick the wrong one and you end up like A Qiang — 5 months in, realizing the direction was never right. Test first, see the timeline, then decide whether to go for it.
