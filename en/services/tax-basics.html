Tax Filing IsSimpler Than You Think

China offers substantial tax benefits for small businesses. No accountant needed, no bookkeeping software required. Read on and you'll understand.

Three Major National Tax Benefits

VAT: Fully Exempt Below ¥100K/Month
Monthly sales revenue under ¥100K (quarterly ¥300K)? Zero VAT. No application needed — automatically waived when you file.
Personal Income Tax: 50% Off on Annual Income ≤ ¥2M
For sole proprietors, taxable profit (revenue minus costs) up to ¥2M/year gets a 50% income tax reduction.
Six Taxes + Two Fees: All Halved
Urban maintenance tax, stamp duty, property tax, urban land use tax, farmland occupation tax, resource tax + education surcharge and local education surcharge — all cut by 50%.

Normal income tax ≈ ¥22,500 → After 50% cut ≈ ¥11,250
That's over ¥10K saved on income tax alone. Add VAT exemption and the six taxes + two fees halving, and the savings are even bigger.

When Do You Need to File Taxes?

Monthly / Quarterly Filing

Most sole proprietors file quarterly (January, April, July, October). Log into the e-tax bureau (etax.chinatax.gov.cn) — it takes just a few minutes.

Annual Reconciliation

Every year, January 1 to March 31, file the annual reconciliation for the previous year's business income. Calculate total annual revenue minus total costs to determine actual profit, then compute the tax owed.

Do You Need to Keep Books?

Sole proprietors can choose between two methods:

  • {Deemed Assessment: Tax bureau sets a "deemed profit rate" based on your industry average. Actual costs don't matter. Best for micro sole proprietors.}
  • {Audited Assessment: Requires accounting records. Tax is calculated on actual revenue minus actual costs. Suitable for larger-scale operations.}

Most one-person companies in the startup phase choose deemed assessment — the simplest process.

Common Questions

Q: Do I need to file if I have no income?
A: Yes. Even with zero income, you must file a "zero return" or risk being flagged as a non-operating entity.
Q: Can I file myself?
A: Absolutely. The e-tax bureau handles everything online. If it feels like too much, accounting firms charge about ¥200-500/month.
Q: What happens if I forget to file?
A: Fines range from ¥200-2,000. Missing 3 consecutive months puts you on the business abnormality list.
