SUCCESS

Shenzhen Renovation Foreman's Subsidy Breakthrough

Lao Zhou, 38 · Interior Renovation Management · Shenzhen

¥2,450/mo
Subsidy received
¥180K
3-month signed deals
5 hrs
OPC time invested

Background

12 years as a renovation foreman in Shenzhen, always operating under someone else's company license. At the end of 2025, the license holder took a 15% cut and delayed payments. In January 2026, he registered his own sole proprietorship — but had no business. Three potential clients were in negotiation but none converted.

Market data: According to Shenzhen Housing Authority's 2025 report, ~4,200 registered renovation sole proprietors in the city. Less than 15% earn ¥50-80K monthly on average; most earn under ¥20K. 90% of renovation foremen operate under a company license, which takes 10-20%.

What OPC Did

  • {Assessment profile — Extremely high execution ability (89%), but channels entirely reliant on old client referrals. Zero online acquisition capability.}
  • {Channel strategy — Skip Xiaohongshu/Douyin. Go WeChat private domain + property management partnerships — 90% of renovation clients come from referrals and property manager recommendations.}
  • {Subsidy application — Shenzhen sole proprietor social insurance subsidy (¥890/mo) + startup rental subsidy (¥1,560/mo), totaling ¥29,400/year. Lao Zhou didn't know these existed.}
  • {Client acquisition — Built a WeChat business card page (name + credentials + completed project photos + client review QR code) to replace traditional paper cards.}

Results

  • {Subsidy: First application approved March 2026, first payment of ¥2,450 arrived in April}
  • {Business: Through property partnerships (10% referral fee), signed 6 new deals in 3 months, totaling ¥180K}
"The subsidy money wasn't the big part, but it made me realize — these things can be done systematically. Before, I just worked with my head down, living day to day."

OPC time invested: 3 hrs material prep + 2 hrs property negotiation script = 5 hrs. Lao Zhou runs it himself from there.

SUCCESS

Shanghai UI Designer's Content Model Turnaround

Xiao Lin, 27 · AI Product UI/UX Design · Shanghai

¥0 → ¥20K/mo
3-month income change
¥3,500
Average deal size
6 hrs
OPC upfront time

Background

UI designer at a Shanghai internet company, laid off at the end of 2025. Severance N+1, ¥120K in savings. Wanted to freelance UI design but sent 30+ portfolios across platforms and only got 2 inquiries — neither converted. Savings burning, scrolling Boss Zhipin and ZBJ every day.

Market data: Q1 2026, 1,200+ new AI startups registered in China (Qichacha). 75% are looking for UI design. Agencies charge ¥50K+ — beyond their budget. Solo UI designers slot right in.

What OPC Did

  • {Assessment profile — Exceptional professional skills (92%), but zero client acquisition and sales ability (23%). Classic craftsman mindset.}
  • {Positioning — Skip ZBJ low-ball gigs. Position: AI product UI design (hottest niche for 2026). Deal size starting at ¥3,500.}
  • {Content model — Topic direction: analyze one AI product UI problem per day (Xiaohongshu + Bilibili). Output cadence: 3 breakdowns + 1 workflow + 1 resource per week. Conversion path: comments → DMs → portfolio PDF → call → proposal.}
  • {Pricing strategy — New client trial at full price but with free icon set; 10% off for repeat; 20% off for monthly retainer.}
  • {AI toolchain — Taught Midjourney + Claude for design drafts, cutting 3 days to 6 hours.}

Results

  • {Month 1: 0 deals (content account building phase — normal)}
  • {Month 2: 8 DM inquiries, 3 deals closed, avg ¥3,500 each}
  • {Month 3: 12 inquiries, 6 deals closed, signed one AI startup on monthly retainer ¥12,000/mo}
  • {Monthly income grew from ¥0 to steady ¥15,000-20,000}

OPC time invested: 3 hrs content model design + 1 hr pricing strategy + monthly reviews (30 min each) = ~6 hrs upfront + 2 hrs/month follow-up.

FAILURE

He Wanted to Be a "Solopreneur Coach" — No One Paid

A Qiang, 32 · Solopreneur Education/Consulting · Chengdu

¥168
Total revenue
¥12,000
Online loan debt
5 months
From startup to quitting

Background

Chengdu. Previously did community operations at a paid knowledge membership group. Quit in early 2026 to become a "solopreneur coach" — teaching others how to run one-person businesses. 80 followers on Zhihu, 200 WeChat contacts, no product. Wanted to charge ¥9,999 for "personal brand coaching."

Market data: China's knowledge-payment market was ¥220B in 2025. "Solopreneur + coach" has 32,000 posts on Xiaohongshu. 90% of "solopreneur coaches" have never actually run a successful one-person business themselves.

Timeline

  • {Month 0: Quit job, posted 3 WeChat Moments: "Coaching ¥9,999/person." Responses: 0}
  • {Month 1: Posted on Zhihu "Top 10 Solopreneur Mistakes." 37 views. Posted 3 articles then stopped.}
  • {Month 2: Started selling ¥9.9 "startup resource pack" (public info scraped from the web). Sold 17 copies, earned ¥168. Two people publicly called him a "韭菜收割机" (hustler preying on newbies).}
  • {Month 3: Started borrowing online loans: ¥3,000 → ¥5,000 → ¥4,000}
  • {Month 4: Canceled business license, started sending resumes}
  • {Month 5: Back to old career doing community operations, ¥8,000/month salary. Never mentioned the word "startup" again.}

Failure Analysis: Why Couldn't OPC Stop This?

Problem What A Qiang Thought Reality
Market selection "Knowledge-pay is hot" Extremely saturated supply. 90% of coaches never succeeded themselves.
Cash flow "All startups go through this" ¥30K savings lasted 3 months. No buffer.
Pricing "I'm worth ¥9,999" 0 followers, 0 products, 0 credentials. Even ¥9.9 was too much.
Execution "But I did the work!" Posted 3 Zhihu articles, stopped. 1 WeChat Moment, stopped.
Attitude toward OPC "What you're saying makes sense" Only listened to what he wanted to hear. Did nothing he didn't want to hear.
"It wasn't your problem. It was mine. I thought knowing how to do it meant I could do it. Turns out, the gap between knowing and doing is a f***ing ocean."

What These 3 Cases Teach Us

  1. {Channel choice beats hard work. Lao Zhou picked the right acquisition path (property partnerships). Xiao Lin picked the right niche (AI product UI). A Qiang picked a niche he wasn't qualified for.}
  2. {Cash reserves are oxygen. ¥30K for 3 months is almost certainly not enough. OPC recommends: save at least 6 months of living expenses before starting.}
  3. {Tools can be given, execution cannot. Same content model — Xiao Lin executed, A Qiang stopped after one post.}
  4. {Do first, then teach. A "coach" with zero hands-on experience is building castles in the air. If you want to do education/consulting — run a real business first.}
  5. {OPC doesn't take people without a product/service foundation. A Qiang's lesson clarified our boundaries: not everyone is suited for independent business. Take the assessment first, then decide.}
