The Biggest Entrepreneurship Story You Haven't Heard

While Silicon Valley debates whether AI will replace jobs, China is quietly engineering the most ambitious small-business policy experiment in history.

In the span of just six months, over 20 Chinese cities have rolled out dedicated policies to support OPCs (One Person Companies) — solo entrepreneurs who use AI tools to run what traditionally required a team of five, ten, or twenty people.

This isn't scattered local experimentation. This is a coordinated, whole-nation push.

From Grassroots Trend to Five-Year Plan

In early 2026, China's 15th Five-Year Plan formally enshrined the "intelligent economy" as a national priority. OPCs — dubbed "super individuals" (超级个体) — were explicitly named as a key vehicle.

The State Administration for Market Regulation released the "China OPC Development Research Report 2026," confirming what policymakers already suspected: OPCs are concentrated in the Yangtze River Delta, Pearl River Delta, and Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei regions, dominated by post-90s and post-00s entrepreneurs. Over 12 million people have already adopted the OPC model.

The response from cities was immediate and competitive.

Three Policy Innovations That Matter Globally

1. "Sandbox Regulation" — Grace Periods for Small Startups

Hangzhou pioneered a regulatory approach that gives OPCs a 1–3 year observation period. During this window, regulators use administrative guidance and risk alerts instead of penalties. The city explicitly prohibits using office size, headcount, or registered capital as barriers to entry or subsidy eligibility.

This is the opposite of "move fast and break things." It's "start small and we'll help you grow."

2. "Workstation Registration" — A Desk Is Enough

Traditional Chinese company registration required a physical commercial address with property deeds and lease agreements. Under new OPC policies, a single desk in a certified OPC community qualifies. No property documents needed. Registration completes in as little as five minutes via mobile app.

3. Data Assets as Registered Capital

Hangzhou and several other cities now allow entrepreneurs to use data assets, intellectual property, and AI models as registered capital contributions. For the first time, a founder can contribute their AI model or dataset as equity — a recognition that in the AI era, data is as valuable as cash.

The Philosophy Behind It

"OPC is not a short-term trend — it's a long-term structural transformation of how society organizes work and economic activity." — Ding Hong, Chinese Academy of Sciences Academician

China's OPC push reflects a clear strategic calculus:

  • Employment pressure: With millions of graduates entering the job market annually, OPCs create a new path to economic participation
  • AI leverage: One OPC with AI tools can replace roughly ¥72 in traditional developer costs for every ¥1 spent on AI (per the 2026 OPC Insight Report)
  • Global competitiveness: The country that builds the best solo-founder ecosystem captures the next generation of AI-native businesses

What This Means for the Rest of the World

China's OPC experiment has implications far beyond its borders:

  1. Policy blueprint: The combination of computing vouchers, sandbox regulation, workstation registration, and entrepreneurship insurance forms a replicable model
  2. Competitive pressure: If China successfully incubates thousands of AI-native solo businesses, it creates a new class of globally competitive micro-enterprises
  3. Talent magnet: Generous subsidies and streamlined processes are already attracting international entrepreneurs to Chinese OPC communities

The message from Chinese policymakers is clear: the future of entrepreneurship is small, AI-powered, and we intend to lead it.
