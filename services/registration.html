The 3-Step Process

1

Prepare Documents

Gather the required documentation for your OPC registration. Requirements vary slightly depending on your city and whether you're a foreign national or Chinese resident.

  • Passport / ID verification
  • Residence permit (for foreigners)
  • Registered address proof (lease or virtual office)
  • Company name (3 backup options)
  • Business scope definition
  • Registered capital declaration
2

Submit & Register

File your application with the local Administration for Market Regulation (AMR). We handle the submission, follow-up, and revisions if needed.

  • Online name approval (1-3 days)
  • Submit registration documents
  • AMR review (5-10 working days)
  • Receive business license
  • Carve official seals
3

Bank Account & Tax

After receiving your license, open a corporate bank account and complete tax registration. This is where many entrepreneurs get stuck — we guide you through it.

  • Open corporate bank account
  • Tax registration & filing setup
  • Apply for eligible subsidies
  • Ongoing compliance support

Requirements for Foreign Nationals

✓

Valid Passport

Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months. Notarized and authenticated copies may be required depending on your city.

✓

Residence Permit

A valid Chinese residence permit is required. Tourist visas are not sufficient for OPC registration.

✓

Registered Address

A commercial or office address in China is required. Virtual office services are available in most cities for freelancers.

✓

Chinese Bank Account

You'll need to open a corporate bank account in China. Some banks require in-person visits for account activation.
